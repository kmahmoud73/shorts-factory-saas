Onboarding Timeline

The target is 14 calendar days from contract signature to first video live on YouTube. Here is the day-by-day breakdown.

Day 0 Contract Signed
Welcome & Kickoff
  • Client receives welcome email with intake form link, OAuth instructions, and onboarding timeline
  • Auto-responder sends intro email from hello@shortsfactory.io
  • Client added to CRM / leads tracker
  • We create your project workspace and assign your account
Day 1-2 Discovery
Intake Call + Form Completion
  • 30-minute video call (Zoom/Google Meet) to walk through the intake form together
  • Discuss niche positioning, content strategy, and competitive landscape
  • Collect all branding assets (logo, banner, colors, fonts, mascot reference)
  • Agree on voice/tone and review sample narrations from our library
  • Confirm upload schedule, approval workflow, and cross-platform distribution preferences
  • Set expectations on timeline and deliverables
  • Client completes remaining form fields after call (deadline: end of Day 2)
Day 3 Authorization
YouTube OAuth + Platform Authorizations
  • Send client the YouTube OAuth authorization link
  • Client clicks link, logs into their Google account, grants upload + analytics permissions
  • Verify token received and test with a private video upload (immediately deleted)
  • Send cross-platform authorization links (Instagram, X, TikTok, etc.)
  • Client generates Bluesky app password, Telegram bot token if applicable
  • We securely store all tokens and configure your pipeline
Day 4-5 Configuration
Pipeline Setup & Branding
  • Configure story templates, compliance rules, editorial scoring weights
  • Process and integrate branding assets (logo, banner, colors, mascot)
  • Set up AI mascot (if requested): generate or process image, configure animation pipeline
  • Configure TTS voice selection and test sample narrations
  • Activate your full automation pipeline (scouting, writing, building, uploading, engagement, monitoring)
  • Configure analytics dashboard and monitoring alerts
  • Run trend scout for client's niche (dry run) to validate topic detection
Day 6-7 Test Build
First Test Video — Internal Review
  • Run full pipeline end-to-end: scout → editor → writer → compliance → builder
  • Build 3 test videos (different topics) to validate quality across variety
  • Internal review: check narration quality, image style, subtitle accuracy, mascot behavior, metadata
  • Fix any issues (common: voice tone mismatch, image style drift, subtitle timing, mascot sizing)
  • Rebuild test videos with corrections
Day 8-9 Client Review
Client Reviews Test Videos
  • Send 3 test videos to client for review (unlisted on YouTube or via direct file)
  • Collect feedback on: voice, visuals, pacing, tone, branding, mascot, overall quality
  • Implement revision requests (typically 1-2 rounds)
  • Client signs off on video quality standard — this becomes the benchmark
Day 10-11 Queue Building
Build Initial Content Queue
  • Run pipeline to generate initial content queue (7-14 videos depending on cadence)
  • Quality check each video against approved benchmark
  • Set up upload queue with staggered scheduling
  • Configure cross-platform distribution for each video
  • Activate engagement bots (comment replier, community poster if eligible)
Day 12 Launch Day
First Video Goes LIVE
  • First video uploaded to YouTube (public)
  • Cross-platform distribution activated
  • Comment replier activated
  • Analytics monitoring begins
  • Client receives confirmation email with video link
  • We verify all systems are running and monitor your first video's performance
Day 13-14 Stabilization
Monitor & Optimize
  • Monitor first 3 videos for performance (views, completion rate, swipe-away rate)
  • Verify all automation running without errors
  • Verify cross-platform posts appearing correctly
  • Verify comment replier tone and quality
  • Send first performance mini-report to client
  • Schedule Week 2 check-in call (15 minutes) to review early results
  • Pipeline transitions to full autonomous mode
Ongoing Steady State
Autonomous Operation + Weekly Reports
  • Pipeline runs fully autonomously: daily scout → write → build → upload → engage
  • Weekly performance report emailed to client every Monday
  • Monthly strategy review call (30 minutes) to adjust niche, topics, or approach
  • Quarterly deep-dive report with channel growth analysis and recommendations
  • Alert notifications for anomalies (AVD drops, view cliffs, suppression detection)
  • Content calendar adjustments based on performance data feedback loop

4C. Revision Policy

  • Test videos (onboarding): Up to 3 rounds of revisions on the first 3 test videos. This is where we lock in the quality standard.
  • Production videos (steady state): Revisions on individual videos are NOT included in the standard workflow. The compliance gate handles quality control automatically.
  • Style adjustments: Changes to voice, tone, visual style, or content strategy are handled during monthly strategy calls and applied to ALL future videos (not retroactively).
  • Emergency pulls: If a video needs to be removed urgently (factual error, legal concern, client request), we remove it within 2 hours during business hours, 12 hours off-hours.
  • Rebuilds: If the pipeline produces a video that clearly fails the agreed quality benchmark, we rebuild it at no additional cost. This applies to technical failures (audio sync issues, rendering artifacts, subtitle errors), not subjective preferences.

4D. Performance Disclaimers

What We Cannot Guarantee

These disclaimers MUST be in the service agreement. They protect both parties and set honest expectations.

  • No view count guarantees. YouTube's algorithm determines reach. We optimize for the algorithm's known preferences (completion rate, hook quality, metadata), but we cannot guarantee specific view counts, subscriber growth, or revenue.
  • No monetization timeline guarantees. Reaching YouTube Partner Program requirements (1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours OR 10M Shorts views) depends on content-market fit, niche competition, and algorithm behavior. Typical timeline: 3-12 months for new channels.
  • No trending guarantees. We cannot guarantee a video will "go viral" or appear on YouTube's trending tab. We can maximize the probability by picking high-scoring topics and optimizing metadata.
  • AI content has inherent limitations. AI-generated images may occasionally have artifacts (extra fingers, text distortion, face inconsistencies). AI narration is high quality but distinguishable from human narration by attentive listeners. We disclose all content as AI-generated per YouTube policy.
  • Algorithm changes are outside our control. YouTube's recommendation algorithm changes without notice. Our algo_intel system monitors for changes weekly, but we cannot prevent or predict algorithm shifts.
  • Third-party API availability. Our pipeline depends on YouTube API, TTS services, image generation APIs, and social media APIs. If any third-party service experiences downtime, delivery may be delayed (our multi-tier fallback chains minimize this risk).
  • Trending content has a shelf life. If the client uses approval workflows that add 24-48 hour delays, trending content may become stale before publishing. We strongly recommend Full Auto mode for trending-focused channels.

Tips for Success

Based on our experience running autonomous YouTube channels, here are the key principles that drive results.

1. Pick ONE Niche and Stay in It

The algorithm rewards niche consistency. A channel about "everything" is a channel about nothing. We configure your pipeline to stay laser-focused on your chosen niche — every trending topic is scored for relevance before it's written.

2. The First 3 Seconds Decide Everything

YouTube Shorts live or die on the "Viewed vs. Swiped Away" metric. You need 70%+ view rate. Our AI story writer is trained to open every video with a hook — a shocking statement, a compelling visual, or an irresistible question — in the first 3 seconds.

3. Consistency Beats Perfection

A channel posting daily with clean animation will outperform a channel posting weekly with premium effects. The algorithm rewards upload frequency and consistency. Our pipeline is designed for daily output — so you never miss a day.

4. Mix in Real Content

If you have real assets — product photos, behind-the-scenes footage, customer testimonials — we can mix them into your pipeline. AI content fills the daily cadence. Real content drives exceptional engagement spikes.

5. Our System Kills Underperformers Automatically

Our performance tracker auto-tags every video by category and tracks views over time. Categories that consistently underperform are automatically deprioritized, while high-performing topics get more attention. Your channel gets smarter every week.

6. 40-60 Seconds is the Sweet Spot

Videos over 40 seconds have 33% higher engagement than shorter clips, while videos under 30 seconds have the highest completion rates. We target the 40-60 second range — long enough for a complete story, short enough for high completion.

7. Every Video Passes Compliance Review

Every video goes through an automated compliance gate before building. This catches policy violations, advertiser-unfriendly language, and metadata issues — protecting your channel from strikes. We maintain a zero-violation track record.

8. Cross-Platform = Free Growth

The same video works on YouTube, Instagram Reels, TikTok, X, Bluesky, Facebook Reels, and Threads. We distribute to every platform you enable — each one brings incremental views and potential subscribers back to YouTube.

9. Never Buy Subscribers or Views

Paid subscribers and views do NOT count toward YouTube Partner Program eligibility. Purchased subscribers also hurt your algorithm performance (YouTube sees a high sub count with low view counts and deprioritizes your content). We build organic growth only.

10. Month 1 Will Be Slow — That's Normal

New channels typically get 10-50 views per video in the first month. This is the algorithm testing your content with small audiences. If the content performs well, distribution widens progressively. Growth compounds. Month 1 is about establishing consistency and letting the algorithm learn.
